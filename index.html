I'm a Ph.D. Student at University of Rome Tor Vergata, in the Network Programmability Laboratory, with Prof. Giuseppe Bianchi.
I received my B.Sc. and M.Sc. from the same university in Electronic Engineering, with a major in CMOS VLSI design and Microwave Systems design.

My main research interests are high-speed hardware architecture design for programmable network devices and domain-specific soft-core processor design. I've worked on offloading architectures for in-kernel network processing functionalities, targetting FPGA-based NICs.
I also serve as Principal Hardware Engineer for Axbryd, our research lab's spin-off startup. At Axbryd, we transform our research milestones into market-ready products, shaping the networks of tomorrow.

In conjunction with my research activity, I co-founded Hyperable, a Lean AI development studio embracing the Lean and Open Innovation movements, lowering the barriers to bleeding-edge Artificial Intelligence technologies, deploying such technologies into the next disruptive products.
